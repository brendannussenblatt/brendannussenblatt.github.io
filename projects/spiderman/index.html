Essential Spider-Man Moments

If you only have time for a few stories, start here. These moments define who Spider-Man is across comics, TV, movies, and animation.

Amazing Fantasy #15 (1962)

First appearance · “With great power…”

Peter Parker gets bitten by a radioactive spider, ignores a crime he could have stopped, and loses Uncle Ben—learning the mantra that shapes every version of Spider-Man.

The Night Gwen Stacy Died (1973)

ASM #121–122 · Green Goblin showdown

Gwen’s death becomes one of Marvel’s most famous tragedies and marks the point where superhero comics start taking permanent consequences seriously.

Black Suit & Venom (1980s)

Secret Wars #8 · ASM #300

The alien symbiote gives Peter a new costume, a darker personality, and eventually becomes Venom—an enemy who knows everything about his life.

Featured characters

Get to know the people who shape Spider-Man’s story across every era.

Hero

Spider-Man (Peter Parker)

The friendly neighborhood hero balancing school, work, and superhero life. From radioactive bite to multiverse adventures, Peter’s choices anchor every decade on this site.

Supporting cast

Mary Jane Watson

Actor, model, and one of Peter’s most important relationships. MJ’s journey tracks Spider-Man’s shift from teen soap opera to adult drama through the ’70s, ’80s, and beyond.

Arch-nemesis

Green Goblin (Norman Osborn)

Businessman, father, and Spider-Man’s most personal enemy. Norman’s schemes drive some of the darkest milestones—from tragedy on the bridge to modern political power plays.
